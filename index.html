About Me

My Background

My name is Jalen, and I’m a computer science and software engineering student who has always been drawn to understanding how things work and why systems behave the way they do. I’ve always liked building things that feel intentional, whether that means breaking something apart to see why it failed or trying to understand the bigger idea behind the problem.

As I got deeper into engineering, I realized how much I enjoy the challenge of situations where the answer isn’t obvious. I like the mix of logic, creativity, pressure, and curiosity that comes with technical work, and I learn best by digging into something and figuring out what actually makes sense in the long run.

My co-op at Cummins showed me what real engineering environments look like. It taught me the value of clear communication when dealing with complicated systems, and it pushed me to write code and documentation that stay understandable even as projects evolve. I also worked on major coding projects built from scratch, which made me more intentional with design decisions and forced me to think about how my choices affect the people I’m working with.

I want to build a career where I’m solving real problems and working on systems that push me to grow. I want to be in places where I can think straight, solve problems without the noise, and work with people who value clear communication. That’s the direction I’m pushing myself toward, and it’s the standard I try to bring into whatever I’m working on.
