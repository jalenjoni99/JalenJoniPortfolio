About Me

My Background

My name is Jalen and I am a computer science and software engineering student who has always been drawn to understanding how things work and why systems behave the way they do. I like building things that feel intentional, whether I am taking something apart to see why it failed or trying to understand the bigger idea behind a problem instead of just memorizing the steps.

As I got deeper into engineering, I realized how much I enjoy the challenge of situations where the answer is not obvious. I like the mix of logic, creativity, and problem solving that comes with technical work. I enjoy debugging complicated issues and connecting smaller parts into one working solution. I learn best by digging into something, testing it, reading documentation, and figuring out why it behaves the way it does.

My internship at Cummins showed me what real engineering environments look like. I worked with system benches, electrical harnesses, actuators, and PCAN tools for diagnostics and flashing. It taught me the importance of clear communication when dealing with complicated hardware and software. It also pushed me to write code and documentation that stay understandable as projects grow and move between teams.

I want to build a career where I am solving real problems and working on systems that push me to grow. I want to be in environments where I can think clearly, solve problems without unnecessary noise, and work with people who value clarity and communication. That is the direction I am moving toward, and it is the standard I try to bring into everything I work on.
